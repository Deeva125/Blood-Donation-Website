Blood Donation

How Blood Donation Helps Patients

Every day, blood donors help people of all ages. Those who are accident and burn victims, have heart surgery and more. In fact, every two seconds someone in the US needs blood.

What is a Blood Donation?

A blood donation occurs when a person voluntarily has their blood draw. This blood goes to people in need of blood and can save many lives.


Advantages of Donating Blood

Before you donate blood, you have to take a simple physical test and blood test before giving blood. This will help identify unkown health concerns. The test will check blood pressure, body temperature, heart rate, and iron levels.

Disadvantages of Donating Blood

Sometimes, a person might experience side effects after donating blood. The temporary reactions that can occur are weakness, dizziness, feeling faint, lightheadedness, nausea, bleeding from the needle, and bleeding under the skin or bruising. These symptons usually dissapear within 24 hours.

Ways you can make a difference

There are many ways you can make a difference, for example Hosting a Blood Drive, Becoming a Volunteer, Donating Blood, and making Donations to services that help with Blood Donations.
